## Restated understanding

The ID Suite is the in-house tooling P5 has built to run the lead-agency model. Five tools split into two categories. **Intelligence and identification** comes from IntelID (weekly briefings), IntentID (intent-audience identification), and VisitorID (anonymous on-site identification). **Execution** comes from SearchID (SEO and GEO content production) and InboxID (automated follow-up to identified prospects).

The tools were previously sold and described as standalone products. They are now framed as the engine that powers a P5 engagement, and the only commercial entry point is the Plan of Record diagnostic. Each tool page describes what the tool does and how the lead agency uses it inside an engagement.

The ID Suite name stays. It is recognizable, it carries SEO equity, and the "ID" in the name now reads as "identification" (IntelID is the one outlier, and that is fine — intelligence is what the agency does with identification).

## Common structure for each tool page

Each of the five tool sub-pages follows the same five-section structure for visual consistency:

1. **Hero.** Tool name + one-line role inside the engagement. Sub-head names what the tool does in concrete terms. No "Schedule a Demo" CTA in the hero.
2. **What the tool actually does.** Two to four short paragraphs of plain language. No marketing speak.
3. **How the lead agency uses it.** One paragraph that places the tool inside the workflow: where the output feeds, what decisions it informs, what other tools it pairs with.
4. **The output, concretely.** What the practice or business owner sees, gets, or reads. Reports, dashboards, content, CRM rows, email drips, etc.
5. **CTA — Begin a Plan of Record.** Standard primary CTA, same as the homepage and the markets umbrella.

Subsections like FAQ, dashboard screenshots, free downloads, and comparison tables can persist on the VisitorID page (which has them today) but are de-emphasized — they live below the main flow, not as the primary content.

Eyebrow numbering across the tool pages follows the toolkit order: 01 IntelID · 02 SearchID · 03 IntentID · 04 VisitorID · 05 InboxID.

## Page 2: `/the-id-suite/intelid/` — IntelID

**Eyebrow:** `01 / INTELID`
**Headline:** `The weekly intelligence read on your market.`
**Sub-head:** `A single Monday-morning briefing on your competitors, your category, and your public reputation. Synthesized from search, social, and review data. In your inbox by 9 AM.`

**What it does (2–3 paragraphs):**
IntelID pulls signal from every channel that matters to your category: competitor search activity, social mentions, review velocity, ad creative changes, press coverage. P5 synthesizes that signal into a short briefing the owner can read in five minutes over coffee.

The briefing is not a dashboard. It is a written read. What changed, what matters, what we are doing about it in the plan this week. It exists because most owners do not have time to keep up with their own market, and most agencies do not bother to read it for them.

**How the lead agency uses it.** IntelID is the weekly input to the plan. Significant moves in the competitor set or the category trigger plan adjustments in the next monthly working session. Most weeks, the briefing confirms the plan is on track.

**The output.** A 600-to-1000-word Monday-morning briefing in your inbox. Plain language. No charts. No KPIs. The thinking, not the data.

**CTA:** Begin a Plan of Record

## Page 3: `/the-id-suite/seo-accelerator/` — SearchID

**Eyebrow:** `02 / SEARCHID`
**Headline:** `The SEO and GEO content engine.`
**Sub-head:** `The execution layer that turns your expertise into the organic traffic your competitors cannot buy. Built on 25 years of P5 SEO legacy and now optimized for generative engine results.`

Note: URL stays `/the-id-suite/seo-accelerator/` for SEO continuity. The page is titled SearchID throughout. Keep "SEO Accelerator" in the meta keywords and the H1 sub-text so search continues to find it.

**What it does (3–4 paragraphs):**
SearchID does three things. Search engine optimization in the traditional sense: technical site architecture, keyword strategy, on-page work, authority building. Generative engine optimization: structuring content so that ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews surface and cite it. Content repurposing: turning one cornerstone piece of content into five-to-ten downstream assets across the channels you already use.

The work compounds. Twelve months in, organic traffic generates qualified leads whether you are running ads that week or not. Eighteen months in, the agency's ad spend is doing less of the heavy lifting because the organic foundation is carrying its share.

**How the lead agency uses it.** SearchID is the execution engine for the content half of the plan. Topics come from the ICP, the IntelID briefing, and the IntentID audience data. The content that ships gets attributed back through VisitorID so the plan knows which pieces are doing the work.

**The output.** Monthly published content (blog posts, service page enhancements, FAQ entries), a monthly SEO performance report, ongoing technical SEO monitoring, GEO structuring on every piece, content repurposed into social, email, and other channels. Quarterly strategy review with the owner.

**CTA:** Begin a Plan of Record

## Page 4: `/the-id-suite/intentid/` — IntentID

**Eyebrow:** `03 / INTENTID`
**Headline:** `The audience layer for paid.`
**Sub-head:** `Custom audiences of people actively researching your services, deployed across Meta, Google, X, TikTok, and LinkedIn.`

**What it does (2–3 paragraphs):**
IntentID builds custom intent audiences using third-party data signals from across the web. The output is a list of real people in your service area who are demonstrably researching what you sell, refreshed weekly and synced into the ad platforms your buyers actually convert on.

Standard demographic targeting wastes money on people who do not need what you sell. IntentID replaces "women aged 35-55 in the Dallas metro" with "people who have read three pieces of content about your category in the last 30 days." Different list, different cost-per-lead, different conversion rate.

**How the lead agency uses it.** IntentID is the audience layer for the paid half of the plan. Lists feed your paid media buyer's campaigns directly. Audiences refresh as the IntelID briefing surfaces shifts in the category. The plan tracks cost-per-lead from IntentID audiences against cost-per-lead from broader audiences as a continuous test.

**The output.** Custom audiences synced to your paid platforms within 48-72 hours of category signal. Weekly refresh. Monthly performance comparison against demographic baselines.

**CTA:** Begin a Plan of Record

## Page 5: `/the-id-suite/visitorid/` — VisitorID

**Eyebrow:** `04 / VISITORID`
**Headline:** `The visitor identification layer.`
**Sub-head:** `Anonymous website visitors identified, scored, and routed to your CRM. The 95 percent of traffic that leaves without filling out a form, finally visible.`

**What it does (3–4 paragraphs):**
Most prospects do not raise their hand on the first visit. They browse your services, compare options, and leave without filling anything out. Traditional analytics tells you a session happened. VisitorID tells you who showed up, what they researched, and how serious they look.

The pipeline runs four stages. Identify. Classify. Score. Deliver. Records are filtered by confidence so bots and junk do not clutter your CRM. Each visitor is tagged by interest area, intent tier, and source. Qualified records arrive in your CRM automatically with the context your team needs to follow up.

U.S. only. Built on a consented identity graph. Compliance configured per vertical (healthcare-adjacent practices get extra care, including HIPAA-adjacent filtering and minimum-data-footprint defaults).

**How the lead agency uses it.** VisitorID is the measurement and attribution layer that closes the loop with sales. The plan reads from VisitorID continuously: which channels are producing qualified visitors, which content is converting, which prospects called and booked. That read feeds back into the plan and rewrites it.

**The output.** A real-time dashboard with intent scoring, traffic sources, and filterable date windows. A high-intent visitors table with names, locations, scores, tier badges, and research interests. Deep visitor profiles with demographic attributes, intent signals, and page-level browsing history. CRM sync with tagged records.

**Existing assets preserved.** The dashboard preview images, the free 97% Problem report, the identity retargeting walkthrough video, and the pitch deck stay on the page. They live below the main flow as deeper-dive resources, not as the primary funnel. The "Schedule a Demo" button is removed in favor of Begin a Plan of Record.

**CTA:** Begin a Plan of Record

## Page 6: `/the-id-suite/inboxid/` — InboxID

**Eyebrow:** `05 / INBOXID`
**Headline:** `Same-day follow-up to the visitors VisitorID identifies.`
**Sub-head:** `Automated retargeting plus behavior-triggered email, fired the day a prospect engages. Warm hand-offs to your team, not cold lists.`

**What it does (2–3 paragraphs):**
InboxID turns visitor identification into action. The day a prospect visits a high-intent page on your site, they enter a behavior-triggered sequence. The retargeting ads keep your brand visible across the platforms they use. The email sequence delivers content tailored to what they engaged with on your site. The hottest prospects flow into your CRM with the context your team needs to pick up the phone.

Same-day matters. Identified prospects who get follow-up within 24 hours convert at materially higher rates than those who get follow-up a week later. InboxID exists because the window between "they showed interest" and "they called your competitor" is shorter than most owners think.

**How the lead agency uses it.** InboxID is the execution layer for the sales-bridge half of the plan. Sequences are tuned to the practice's intake process and the conversion patterns VisitorID is reporting. The plan tracks open rates, click-through, and identified-to-booked conversion as continuous reads.

**The output.** Same-day automated email sequences. Retargeting audiences refreshed daily. CRM rows with engagement history. Monthly performance summary tied to bookings, not impressions.

**CTA:** Begin a Plan of Record
